# Voice & Tone

Source: Figma file `r20p8eotJkuTxjmSXiYp0n`, node `11181:351` ("Voice Qualities" + "Styling & Formatting").

## Voice — what we sound like

Glints copy is built around four voice statements. Every piece of UX text should reflect at least one.

### 1. We use simple words and phrases to convey big feelings.
**Qualities:** *Brief · Excited · Pushing · Active voice · Direct*

- Short over long. One idea per sentence.
- Active voice ("Apply now") over passive ("Your application can be submitted").
- Pushing toward action — every screen has a clear next step.

### 2. We bring creativity to the conversation but still remain professional.
**Qualities:** *Jolly · Creative*

- Playful word choices when context allows, but never silly.
- Professional defaults: dates, jobs, salaries are factual; surrounding copy can be lighter.

### 3. We're the cheerleader to cheer up users.
**Qualities:** *Supportive · Gender-neutral · Clear*

- Encouraging: "Almost there!", "You've got this", "Just one more step".
- Gender-neutral throughout: "they / their", not "he/she".
- Clear over clever — never sacrifice comprehension for tone.

### 4. We are interested and knowledgeable. Backed with real data.
**Qualities:** *Evidence-based · Universal · Interested*

- Make claims you can back up with data; cite stats when helpful.
- Universal phrasing — works across regions, age groups, industries.
- Curious tone — show interest in the user's career, not just the transaction.

## Styling & Formatting — the mechanical rules

### Numbers
- Write all numbers **numerically**, even numbers under 10 (`5 jobs`, not `five jobs`).
- If a number **starts a sentence**, spell it out — or rewrite the sentence so it doesn't start with the number (especially if `>999`).
- For numbers `>999`, include a comma: `1,000 applications` not `1000 applications`.

### Capitalization
- **Sentence case** for headers, subheads, blog post titles, page titles: *"Find your next job"* — only the first word capitalized.
- **Title case** for job titles and personal names: *"Software Engineer"*, *"Sarah Wu"*.
- **ALL CAPS** for buttons: *"APPLY NOW"*, *"CHAT NOW"*.
- After a colon: **lowercase if a fragment**, **capitalize if a complete sentence**.

### Punctuation
- Don't punctuate headlines, except with an exclamation point: *"Welcome!"* but not *"Welcome."*.
- Don't punctuate subheads unless with `!`. Multi-sentence subheads get punctuation per sentence.
- **Never** use punctuation in a button.
- **Hyphen (`-`)** to join two words into a compound adjective: *"work-from-home"*, *"part-time"*.
- **En dash (`–`)** with **no space** on either side, for ranges: *"5–10K"*, *"Mon–Fri"*.
- **Avoid em dashes (`—`)** entirely.
- Ellipsis: no spaces around it inside a sentence; add a space if it ends the sentence.

### Lists
- Introduce a list with a **colon**.
- Don't capitalize or punctuate list items consisting of phrases or single words.

### Symbols
- **Avoid `&`** as a replacement for "and" in body copy. Exceptions: ads or character-limited contexts (push titles, buttons).
- **Serial comma** is optional — pick one and stay consistent.

### Emoji
Useful for grabbing attention. Allowed in:
- Push notifications
- Email subjects
- Social media

Place at the **beginning or end** of the title or body line — not mid-sentence.

### URLs & emails
- Leave off `http://` and `www.`.
- All URLs in **lowercase**.
- All email addresses in **lowercase**.

### Bold
- Use **bold** to refer to a UI element or action: *"Tap **Apply** to submit your application."*

## Quick reference — Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| `5 jobs` | `five jobs` |
| `1,000 applications` | `1000 applications` |
| `Find your next job` (sentence case header) | `Find Your Next Job` |
| `APPLY NOW` (button caps) | `Apply Now` / `Apply now` (button) |
| `5–10K` (en dash, no space) | `5 - 10K` / `5—10K` (em) |
| `work-from-home` (hyphen for compound adj) | `work — from — home` |
| `Tap **Apply** to continue` | `Tap "Apply" to continue` |
| `they / their` | `he/she` |
| `glints.com/jobs` | `https://www.Glints.com/Jobs/` |

## Why it matters

The user-facing copy is part of the product. Inconsistent capitalization, mixed dashes, or formal-vs-playful drift makes the app feel less polished. When generating copy for a screen, run it through this checklist before pushing.
